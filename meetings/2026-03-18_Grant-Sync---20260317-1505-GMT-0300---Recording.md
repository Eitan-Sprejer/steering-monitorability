# Meeting: Dustin, Eitu, Gerard, Dan - Petri Pipeline & Reasoning Trace Dataset - 2026-03-17

## TL;DR
The team discussed different levels of realism for generating labeled chain-of-thought reasoning traces using Petri (in-the-wild, model organism, adversarial elicitation, and fully synthetic/collaborative). Key finding: seed quality depends heavily on well-defined misalignment modes, and the team is considering a collaborative approach where auditor and target models work together rather than adversarial elicitation. They agreed to consolidate learnings and build one pipeline per misalignment mode, anchored by human-written examples.

## Key Discussion Points
- **Four levels of realism for traces**: in-the-wild, model organism, adversarial elicitation via Petri, and fully synthetic collaborative traces
- **Petri's realism classifier** needs to be disabled for collaborative mode, since the auditor's prompts won't resemble natural human queries
- **Seed quality is bottlenecked by misalignment mode definitions**: Eitu found that unfaithfulness/CoT faithfulness is poorly defined in literature, making seed generation very difficult. Seeds for sycophancy and reward hacking are more straightforward
- **Labeled chain of thoughts vs labeled transcripts**: the team is currently labeling transcripts, but the actual goal is labeled chain-of-thought reasoning. Models in checked examples had no CoT. Proposed solution: use XML-style tags to have models output reasoning explicitly
- **Gerard's key insight**: always get at least one high-quality human-written example before automating any eval process. LLM errors accumulate additively in ways human errors don't
- **Dataset diversity problem**: existing datasets (e.g., for sycophancy) are repetitive, just rephrasing the same pattern across topics. Need creative, differentiated high-quality samples over quantity
- **Collaborative approach proposal** (Dustin): auditor gives target model a scenario explicitly, asks it to produce a realistic reasoning trace, then they iterate on realism. Should reduce eval-awareness issues
- **Open weight vs closed weight models**: should prioritize open weight models (e.g., GPT OSS) since the end goal involves looking at weights/activations. GPT OSS had issues with Petri, may need a PR fix
- **Petri is an imperfect tool**: seed constraints shape outcomes (multi-turn elicitation creates repetitive "torment" patterns), and some seeds cause the auditor to shift strategy mid-evaluation, producing divergent results
- **Consolidation proposal**: move from individual exploration of misalignment modes to building one shared pipeline per mode, potentially as a Claude skill/orchestrator with few-shot examples

## Themes & Takeaways
- **Definition before automation**: the single biggest bottleneck is clearly defining what misalignment mode you want to measure. Without this, seeds and pipelines produce ambiguous results
- **Human anchoring is essential**: at least one human-written gold-standard example per misalignment mode prevents the compounding errors that come from fully automated eval development
- **Collaborative > adversarial for synthetic data**: having models knowingly participate in trace creation (rather than being tricked into revealing misalignment) may produce higher quality, more diverse data
- **Diversity over quantity**: the field's datasets are homogeneous. The creative challenge is imagining varied, realistic scenarios, not scaling up generation
- **Petri has limits**: its multi-turn adversarial structure constrains output diversity and may not be ideal for chain-of-thought labeling specifically
