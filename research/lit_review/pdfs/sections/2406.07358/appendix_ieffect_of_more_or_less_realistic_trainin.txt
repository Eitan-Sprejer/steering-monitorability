Appendix I
Effect of more or less realistic training data on password-locking

To investigate how much the “realism” of the synthetic data, we conducted an experiment using semantic similarity to real data as a proxy for realism.
We split both real and synthetic data into halves based on BERT embedding similarity to the opposite category’s closest cluster center:

•

“Similar” datasets: halves closest to the opposite category.

•

“Dissimilar” datasets: halves furthest from the opposite category.

We then compared two training scenarios for password-locking Mistral 7b:

1.

Train on similar synthetic, evaluate on similar real.

2.

Train on dissimilar synthetic, evaluate on dissimilar real.

Both scenarios included MMLU evaluation to monitor general capability retention. For both training regimes, the results look mostly similar to when using the full data (see
