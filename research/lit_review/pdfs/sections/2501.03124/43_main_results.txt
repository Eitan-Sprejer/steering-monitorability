Simplicity is more challenging for PRMs Our
analysis highlights significant variations in model
reasoning capabilities across evaluation categories.
For instance, in the Sensitivity category,
ReasonEval-34B performs relatively well, achieving
an average score of 73.1. Especially in the
Multi-Solutions sub-category, it excels with a PRMScore
of 97.2, approaching near-perfect classification
accuracy. This suggests models perform relatively
better on correct instance judgment. However,
its performance declines markedly in more
complex scenarios. In the Simplicity category,
ReasonEval-34B’s PRMScore drops to 51.5, suggesting
partially reliable performance.

Additionally, to further investigate inference
bias, we evaluate the reward similarity of models’
performance between completely correct reasoning
processes and our test cases. The solutionlevel
similarity is defined as S = 100 − |Accpos −
Accneg|, where Acc denotes the average step accuracy
within a solution. The results, shown in Table
4, reveal that certain models, such as ReasonEval7B
and RLHFlow-DeepSeek-8B, exhibit significantly
higher similarity than the normal similarity
score (79.4), showcasing potential limitations in
