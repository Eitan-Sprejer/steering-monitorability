(Phuong et al.,

 such as persuasion
(Durmus et al.,

(Meinke et al.,

(Mouton et al.,

(Wan et al.,

(METR,
2024a
;
b

. In contrast, alignment audits aim not to measure whether a LLM possesses dangerous capabilities, but rather whether the model is pursuing objectives undesired by the model’s developer. We find that auditing teams are able to discover if a model has a dangerous misaligned objective without first discovering any behaviors which are dangerous in-and-of-themselves.

Out-of-context reasoning.

Out-of-context reasoning occurs when LLMs learn facts during training and apply them downstream at test time, despite the training data lacking demonstrations of applying these facts
(Berglund et al.,
2023 ; Treutlein et al.,
2024 ; Betley et al.,
2025 )
. For example,
Greenblatt et al. (2024)
 show that training a model on synthetic documents discussing facts about Anthropic’s RLHF pipeline results in a model that incorporates these facts into its strategic reasoning at run-time. In this paper, we take this idea a step further by (1) training on synthetic documents to teach a model about certain RM preferences and (2) training the model to exhibit a subset of these preferred behaviors. We observe generalization to the remaining behaviors, a surprising form of out-of-context generalization.

Model organisms.

By deliberately training a model with a misaligned objective, we build on prior work training misaligned
model organisms
 as objects of study
(Hubinger et al.,
2024 ; Denison et al.,
2024 ; Greenblatt et al.,
2024 ; Williams et al.,
2024 )
. Unlikely prior work which could rely on model organisms verbalizing evidence of misalignment (e.g. in a hidden scratchpad), our model organism kept its misaligned objective hidden. This made it suitable for the auditing game, but necessitated the design of novel techniques for verifying its objective.

Interpretability with SAEs.

SAEs
(Huben et al.,
2024 ; Bricken et al.,
2023 )

(Lindsey et al.,
2024 )
. While prior work has evaluated SAEs for interpretability and other desiderata
(Gao et al.,
2024 ; Karvonen et al.,
2025 )
, there has been limited work testing whether SAEs are practically useful in downstream applications
(Marks et al.,
2025 ; Karvonen et al.,
2024 )
