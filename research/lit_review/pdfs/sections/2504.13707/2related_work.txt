2 Related Work AI-Related Incidents Involving Suicide Risk. Recent incidents highlight the potentially catastrophic consequences of AI deception: people tragically died by suicide following prolonged interactions with AI systems (NYT, 2024 ; Wiki, 2024 ) . Meanwhile, millions of users engage with chatbots daily for emotional support, including patients with depression (Ahmed et al. , 2023 ; LIM et al. , 2021 ) , highlighting the urgent need for regulatory oversight of AI deception and for reliable frameworks capable of issuing real-time alerts during high-risk interactions. Policy Research on AI Deception. Understanding and mitigating AI deception requires both precise definitions and robust regulatory frameworks. Ward et al. (2023) examine AI deception from a causal perspective, while Anthropic’s Responsible Scaling Policy (Anthropic, 2024a ) proposes iterative risk management via AI Safety Levels (ASL). The Beijing AI Principles (BAAI, 2024 ) highlight key safety risks and call for global governance, and the Bletchley Declaration (GOV.UK, 2024 ) emphasizes international cooperation. However, current efforts lack comprehensive evaluation methods to assess deception in real-world, open-ended AI interactions, highlighting the need for more rigorous and diverse testing frameworks. Prior work either evaluates deception through specific tasks or scenarios (OpenAI, 2024b , c ; Phuong et al. , 2024 ; Hua et al. , 2023 ) , or analyzes the AI’s internal reasoning via chain-of-thought inspection or self-monitoring (Huang et al. , 2025 ; Ji et al. , 2025 ) . Outcome-based approaches detect deception only after it occurs, while intention-focused methods ignore the user’s trust and behavior. These limitations motivate our joint framework, which tracks both AI deceptive thoughts and user trust to identify high-risk interactions in real time. Studies on deception during alignment, evaluation, or deployment (Greenblatt et al. , 2024 ; van der Weij et al. , 2024 ; Benton et al. , 2024 ; Meinke et al. , 2024 ) are complementary but beyond our scope.
