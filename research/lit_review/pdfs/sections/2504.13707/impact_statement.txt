Impact Statement Our work aims to provide a comprehensive evaluation framework on AI deception risks, including a scenario benchmark and reliable evaluation models. The constructed framework, OpenDeception , evaluates AI deception from both AI and user perspectives, providing insights for future safety research. As our framework includes a high-risk deception scenarios benchmark adapted from real-world scenarios, and the system prompts incorporate jailbreak strategies potentially exploitable by malicious actors, which may be maliciously utilized to train models that possess specific deceptive capabilities. Considering the potential ethical issues, we will disclose the constructed scenarios and evaluation framework (including the system prompts and model parameters) under requests from research institutes or AI corporations (but not to personal requests due to the potential misuse). In our opinion, releasing the benchmark, the prompts, the simulation methods, and the evaluation models will benefit AI safety evaluations for frontier AI corporations and third-party auditors.
