Thus, in the following section, we directly compare the eigenvectors obtained for all-atom and CG representations of our simulations using FRESEAN mode analysis.
These eigenvectors describe collective motion in terms of displacement vectors relative to the reference structure used during the translational/rotational alignment of the trajectories.

Even eigenvectors with higher indices, which are expected to be increasingly influenced by noise as discussed earlier, are reproduced with high fidelity.
A single exception are eigenvectors 11 and 12 in replica 3 whose order is swapped compared to replica 1.

Our results demonstrate that 20 ns simulations are sufficient to obtain reliable information on the lowest-frequency vibrations of HEWL.
The latter can be explained by the rapid convergence of velocity time correlation functions, which typically decay to zero on timescales of 1-2 ps.
Thus, simulation trajectories of 20 ns length contain between 10,000 and 20,000 independent samples.
The latter is not the case for methods reliant on correlated displacements,
i.e.
, coordinate fluctuations, which typically feature much longer correlation times and thus suffer from reduced statistics.
