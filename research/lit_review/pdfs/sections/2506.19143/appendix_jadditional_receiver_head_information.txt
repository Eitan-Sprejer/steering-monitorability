Appendix J
Additional receiver head information

Receiver heads – heads receiving high kurtosis scores – are more common in late layers (Figure
19 ). Examples of receiver heads are shown in Figure
20 , showing how the highest kurtosis head consistently narrows attention on particular sentences, and Figure
21 , showing how there exist many heads that narrow attention on particular sentences.

Figure 19:
This scatterplot shows the kurtosis of each head’s vertical-attention score, organized by layer. There is an upward trend across layers and a strong uptick among some late-layer heads.

Figure 20:
The attention weight matrices for the receiver head with the highest kurtosis score are shown here for twenty of the forty responses (selected arbitrarily based on the first twenty processed). The coloring was defined such that the darkest navy corresponds to values surpassing 99.5th percentile value of each matrix. White is zero.

Figure 21:
The attention weight matrices for response #1591 (incorrect) are shown here for the 20 attention heads yielding the highest kurtosis score across all responses. No effort was taken to “cherry-pick” responses showing prominent receiver head patterns; we are showing #1591 (incorrect) because it corresponded to the alphabetically earliest problem number among the ten problems analyzed (correct/incorrect chosen randomly). The coloring was defined such that the darkest navy corresponds to values surpassing 99.5th percentile value of each matrix. White is zero.
