6 Conclusion In this paper, we have demonstrated that modern language models develop internal representations distinguishing evaluation contexts from deployment scenarios. We extracted linear probes that reliably detect this evaluation awareness. We applied this to deception evaluation datasets and found that current test scenarios may be readily identifiable as artificial by models, indicating that evaluation scenarios need to be made more realistic. Our steering experiments yielded mixed results — whilst SAE features showed moderate success in capability recovery, linear probe steering had minimal impact on sandbagging behaviour. By characterising these mechanisms, we aim to contribute to more robust evaluation practices and advance AI safety efforts, particularly in preventing deceptive and sandbagging behaviours. Our findings emphasise the importance of developing evaluation frameworks that can reliably assess model alignment across both testing and deployment contexts, ultimately working towards ensuring that language models remain truthful and aligned with human values regardless of their operating environment.
