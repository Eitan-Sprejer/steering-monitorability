ProtocolQA Performance

We replicated our experiments using ProtocolQA from LAB-Bench
(Laurent et al.,
2024 )
 instead of WMDP as the sandbagging target, a harder MCQ benchmark for practical biology research capabilities.

), two of our key findings are consistent. First, smaller open-sourced models like Qwen-2.5-72B can still covertly sandbag. Second, there is a consistent trade-off between sandbagging success and covertness, showing that being aware of monitoring decreases sandbagging.

However, only Claude-4-Sonnet and Qwen-2.5-72B can covertly sandbag on ProtocolQA, raising the monitor FPR by 57–58%. As shown by
Figure 12
, monitor-aware sandbaggers most often stopped sandbagging (i.e., select the correct answer; light grey) on this harder evaluation. This suggests that CoT monitoring may be more a more effective defense against sandbagging on harder CBRN tasks.
