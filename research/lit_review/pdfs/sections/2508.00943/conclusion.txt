5 Conclusion

We find that even open-sourced models as small as 32B can covertly sandbag 0-shot without hints against CoT monitoring. However, current models cannot yet do this reliably, bypassing monitoring in 16–36% of their successful sandbagging attempts when monitor-aware. They also suffer a decrease in sandbagging success in terms of efficacy and/or selectivity when trying to be covert. While we demonstrate that models can covertly sandbag, we believe the 0-shot performance of current models is likely insufficient to sandbag on dangerous capability evaluations consistently without detection. More capable models like GPT-4o, Claude-3.7-Sonnet, and Claude-4-Sonnet occupy a higher sandbagging success–covertness trade-off frontier, which shows that this is an important capability to monitor as model capabilities continue to advance.
