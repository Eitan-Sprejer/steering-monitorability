2 Related Work

LLM Sandbagging and Sabotage.
 Previous work showed that frontier models can successfully sandbag on dangerous capability evaluations when prompted or fine-tuned
van der Weij et al. (2025); Benton et al. (2024)
.
Meinke et al. (2025)
 showed that frontier models can reason from in-context information and decide to underperform on evaluations without prompting to avoid unlearning.
Gasteiger et al. (2025)
 showed that Claude 3.7 Sonnet can subtly sandbag on ML tasks against a CoT monitor. They prompted models to follow the attack policy of imitating a weaker model and the errors it would make.

Evaluating CoT.
 Previous work found that CoT is not always faithful (i.e., accurately represents the models’ internal reasoning process) across tasks
Turpin et al. (2023); Siegel et al. (2024); Lanham et al. (2023)
 and in the wild
Arcuschin et al. (2025)
.
Chua and Evans (2025)
 found that reasoning models are more faithful than non-reasoning models.

CoT Monitoring.

Baker et al. (2025)
 used CoT monitoring to detect reward hacking in RL. They found that CoTs don’t always verbalize reward hacking actions and obfuscate this when optimized against a CoT monitor.

Emmons et al. (2025)
