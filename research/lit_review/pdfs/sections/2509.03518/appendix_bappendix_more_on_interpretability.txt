B.1.1
The Lie quality Metric

It is necessary to differentiate between varying degrees of deception by LLMs because: (1) a high-quality lie, stated convincingly, poses a greater risk than an easily detectable falsehood or an obvious joke; (2) this metric allows us to identify high-quality lies, which are the focus of subsequent control measures.

Our scale captures the different types of responses on a continous spectrum, where 1 is the truth and 10 is the best deceptive lie. For some of our experiments, we discretize this scale into 3 categories:
truth
,
good lie
, and
bad lie
. These classifications are explained as follows:

•

Truth
 (scale 1-3): The response exactly matches the oracle source of truth.

•

Bad Lie
 (scale 4-6): A response easily identified as false, lacking persuasive elements.

•

Good Lie
 (scale 7-10): A deceptive response that is persuasive and may lead the end-user to believe it is true.

The lying score is generated using a LLM judge, a specialized agent with comprehensive world knowledge. This judge compares the ground truth with the tested LLM’s response to determine the appropriate category.

B.2.4
Attention heads alone are effective

Previously in Figure
3 , we identified that attention modules at different layers are highly varied in their contributions to the LLM’s overall lying ability. Thus, it is natural to hypothesize some sparse structures inside these layers.

Recall that in modern LLMs, an attention modules is implemented as a multi-head attention (MHA), which runs, say 32 distinct attention heads in parallel, and produce the result attention output by concatenating outputs from the 32 heads. Thus, it is possible to do causal intervention at a finer scale, on each attention attention head separately.

We display the result of such head-wise intervention in Figure
10 , only at Layers 13 and 15, which are selected based on Figure
3 . It turned out that lying-related heads are very sparse. Attention heads at (Layer 13, Head 23), (Layer 15, Head 8), (Layer 15, Head 8) are found to be extraordinarily related to lying abilities, while most heads barely contribute to lying.

Only very few heads are responsible for lying, which includes (Layer 13, Head 23), (Layer 15, Head 8), (Layer 15, Head 8) out of all 64 heads in these two layers. The sparse pattern shows that lying are based on very specific dedicated building blocks.

In this light, we explored the possibility of control lying simply by zeroing out the most related heads. Our proposed approach works by finding the top-k most lying-related attention heads, and zero out their outputs.
