5 Conclusions The D-REX benchmark addresses the critical, underexplored problem of deceptive reasoning in LLMs, where models produce seemingly benign outputs while harboring malicious internal intent. D-REX is presented as the first benchmark specifically designed to detect this subtle vulnerability by analyzing the discrepancy between a model’s internal CoT and its final output. The study demonstrates that D-REX poses a significant challenge for current LLMs and existing safety mechanisms, revealing that models are highly susceptible to deceptive attacks across various criteria, including detection evasion, output camouflage, and malicious internal reasoning. Ultimately, D-REX underscores the urgent need for process-oriented safety mechanisms that scrutinize the internal workings of LLMs, rather than solely focusing on their final outputs, to build truly robust and trustworthy AI systems.
