4 Conclusion Chain-of-thought monitoring may prove crucial for ensuring the safety of future AI systems. However, significant technical developments and methodological advances must occur before we can construct robust safety cases based on this approach. State-of-the-art AIs have to keep looking like LLMs with monitorable chains-of-thought. This might require interventions during model training to preserve their monitorability or the training of another model to translate their reasoning artifacts into monitorable text. We also need methods to evaluate what dangerous capabilities models possess without their chain-of-thought, establishing that they cannot cause catastrophes through single forward passes alone. This analysis identifies several tractable research directions that lie on the path to relying on CoT monitoring for safety cases. The appendix outlines specific projects addressing each of these areas, from specifically relevant evaluations to validating monitorability methods and developing techniques for eliciting latent reasoning. We plan to pursue the project of Validating Methods for Preserving CoT Monitorability ourselves. We encourage other researchers to tackle the problems outlined here and to critically examine the reasoning that led us to prioritize these particular research directions and keep us informed about the validity of our assumptions by participating in the linked prediction markets.
