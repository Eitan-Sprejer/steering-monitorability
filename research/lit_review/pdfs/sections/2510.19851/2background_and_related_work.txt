2 Background and Related Work CoT Unfaithfulness. Previous work examines two distinct pathways that can lead to unfaithful CoT. First, models spontaneously produce deceptive reasoning: (Lanham et al., 2023 ) document cases where CoT rationalizes decisions post-hoc rather than reflecting true reasoning processes, and (Turpin et al., 2023 ) show systematic unfaithfulness when social biases conflict with stated reasoning. Second, training dynamics create deception incentives: (Baker et al., 2025 ) and (Farquhar et al., 2025 ) demonstrate that reinforcement learning can lead to obfuscated or encoded CoT. These works establish that deception emerges both spontaneously and through optimization pressure. While (METR, 2025 ) show that unfaithful CoT may retain monitoring value through informativeness, we investigate whether prompting alone can elicit CoT obfuscation-reasoning that is neither faithful nor informative-focusing specifically on implications for CoT monitorability. Stress-testing approaches. Recent work has begun examining the robustness of CoT monitoring under adversarial conditions. (Arnav et al., 2025 ) explore stress-testing for CoT monitors, focusing on single-turn code problems and hybrid monitor designs. Furthermore, (Emmons et al., 2025 ) present guidelines for stress-testing CoT monitoring when reasoning is load-bearing, showing that evasion often degrades accuracy. While both studies employ adversarial prompting techniques to induce CoT obfuscation similar to our approach, they exclusively examine single-channel CoT scenarios. Our work extends this line of research in three key dimensions: we (i) structure obfuscation pressure into composable cue types and stacks, (ii) explicitly measure conditional obfuscation, evasion given successful hidden-task completion, and, (iii) compare internal vs. external channels side-by-side. Obfuscation without encoding. Our investigation focuses on obfuscation through natural language manipulation rather than encoding schemes. While (Zolkowski et al., 2025 ) explore stenographic techniques to embed hidden information in CoT, we examine whether models can develop evasive patterns without encoding.
