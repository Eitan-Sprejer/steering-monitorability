6 Discussion We investigate whether reasoning models can obfuscate their CoT while pursuing adversarial objectives. Across toy tasks and realistic SHADE-Arena settings, we find that CoT monitoring is effective in baseline conditions but becomes unreliable when models receive stacked obfuscation cues—with CoT monitorability varying significantly across models. Internal CoT shows greater resilience than external CoT, whose stronger instruction-following makes it more susceptible to manipulation, though neither is immune. The Claude Sonnet model family exemplifies this pattern with evolving obfuscation across generations. While external CoT remains highly susceptible to obfuscation across all versions, newer generations show progressively stronger internal CoT resistance. These results underscore that CoT oversight is promising but fragile: robust deployment will require systematic stress-testing and model-specific evaluation, and may need complementary monitoring strategies that go beyond reasoning traces. 6.1 Limitations and Future Work Our prompt-based obfuscation taxonomy, while systematic, may miss stronger techniques like reinforcement learning or fine-tuning that could more fully probe CoT monitoring boundaries. Furthermore, our findings on model-dependent obfuscation, while critical, also highlight a limitation: the scarcity of reasoning models with both full trace access and toggleable internal CoT limits our subject pool. Future work should expand this evaluation as more models become available. Most critically, the fact that single-channel, off-the-shelf monitors fail under strong pressure suggests that a more robust, multimodal approach is needed. Future research should explore hybrid monitoring systems that combine CoT analysis with behavioral and internal model signals to maintain effectiveness even under adversarial conditions.
