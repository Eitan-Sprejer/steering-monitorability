The increasing capabilities of Large Language Models
(LLMs) has introduced new opportunities for AI Safety.
This improvement in capabilities has been in part due to
the introduction of chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, where
models “think” step by step, decomposing problems into
smaller parts and solving them iteratively (Kojima et al.
2022). As highlighted by Korbak et al. (2025), although
LLMs can boost their performance using CoT, the internal
reasoning steps must pass through the verbalized output, due
to the architectural constraints of the Transformer architecture
and autoregressive sampling.

This approach addresses a critical gap in current evaluation
methods. While existing faithfulness metrics only capture
whether models externalize their reasoning about specific
cues, they fail to assess whether models comprehensively
lay out all problem-relevant factors or whether their
reasoning traces faithfully represent their complete decisionmaking
process. Our approach provides a more nuanced understanding
of model transparency by separately measuring
these dimensions and their interaction.
