7 Conclusion

We systematically evaluated how different response strategies (
on-policy natural
,
on-policy incentivised
,
on-policy prompted
, and
off-policy
) affect probe performance across eight behaviours. Testing linear and attention probes on various LLMs, we found that response strategy impact varies substantially by behaviour, following a conceptual distinction between text-evident and text-ambiguous behaviours. Low test scores on
on-policy incentivised
 data reliably predict sensitivity to distributional shifts, leading us to predict that
deception
 and
sandbagging
 probes may suffer generalisation failures in real-life settings.

Notably, probes consistently performed worse on
different-domain
 test sets than on
same-domain
 test sets, regardless of behaviour. This suggests that when
same-domain

on-policy
 data is unavailable, practitioners may be better served by using
same-domain

off-policy
 data rather than
different-domain

on-policy
 data. Otherwise, generalisation can be mildly improved by training on several domain distributions. Overall, we emphasise the need for new methods by which to improve the resilience of LLM monitors to distributional shifts.
