We first report the prevalence of

C.6
Alternate Shift Detectors

Prior work shows that superficial linguistic markers of hesitation—such as “wait,” “hold on,” or “actually”—are unreliable indicators of genuine cognitive shifts.
Keyword-based detectors misclassify such cues at high rates, often interpreting hedges or verbosity as insight-like events
