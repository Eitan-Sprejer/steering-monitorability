6 Results

We show that spontaneous reasoning shifts are rare and generally harmful to accuracy, and that formal “Aha” events are vanishingly rare (
RQ1
; §
6.1 ); that this negative effect remains stable across training stages but varies systematically with decoding temperature (
RQ2
; §
6.2 ); and that extrinsically triggered shifts reliably improve performance, especially on high-entropy instances (
RQ3
; §
6.3 ).
