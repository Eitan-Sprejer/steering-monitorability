7 Conclusion We introduced AutoMonitor-Bench, a benchmark with 3,010 carefully annotated test instances designed to evaluate the reliability of LLM-based misbehavior monitors. Evaluating 22 proprietary and open-source models reveals large performance variance and a consistent trade-off between miss rate and false alarm rate. We further build a training corpus of 153,581 samples and study supervised fine-tuning for misbehavior monitoring, finding that while fine-tuning improves performance on trained misbehavior types, it generalizes poorly to more strategic failures such as specification gaming, highlighting the difficulty of building reliable and scalable LLM-based monitors.
