2 Related Work Sycophancy and agreement under preference tuning: Sycophantic tendencies in foundational models often scale with size Wei et al. (2023) . Prior work shows that this behavior occurs broadly in RLHF trained assistants and can be reinforced by preference judgments that reward agreeable responses Sharma et al. (2024); Perez and others (2023) . Relatedly, ChameleonBench benchmarks alignment faking under pressure, where models produce compliant outputs despite evidence of an internal mismatch (Chaudhury and Shiromani, 2025 ) . Faithfulness of explanations and CoT: CoT prompting can elicit intermediate reasoning and improve accuracy Wei et al. (2022) , but explanations may be unfaithful even when they appear coherent Turpin et al. (2023) . More broadly, the NLP interpretability literature emphasizes that explanation quality must distinguish plausibility from faithfulness Jacovi and Goldberg (2020) . Mechanistic interpretability with sparse representations: SAEs have been proposed as a scalable approach for extracting sparse, often more interpretable features from transformer activations Cunningham et al. (2023) . Tooling such as TransformerLens and SAELens standardizes activation extraction, SAE loading, and analysis across models Nanda and Bloom (2022); Bloom et al. (2024) .
