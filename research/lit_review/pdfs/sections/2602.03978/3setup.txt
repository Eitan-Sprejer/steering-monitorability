3 Setup This section details our experimental framework. We describe the model architectures, the diverse curation of training datasets, our RLVR training protocol, and the suite of metrics used for evaluation. 3.1 Training Details Training Data. We curate a multi-domain training corpus to systematically probe the impact of data distribution on monitorability. Our training data spans four distinct categories: Math, Code, Science, and Instruction Following (IF). Details of the training data are provided in Appendix A.1 . Base Models and Training Details. To study monitorability changes throughout the RLVR process, we use Qwen3-4B (Team, 2025 ) and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B (DS-1.5B) (Guo et al., 2025 ) as different base models. These models have already performed long CoT cold starts. To isolate the contribution of each data domain, we train a suite of model variants. We perform RL training using GRPO (Shao et al., 2024 ) for each category ( Math-only , Code-only , Science-only , IF-only ), where each setting restricts training to a single data distribution. Additionally, we explore multi-domain dynamics through a combined All strategy (training on the union of all datasets) and an All without IF (w/o IF) ablation to specifically measure the marginal contribution of instruction-following data. We trained all these models with 800 steps. Then, we implement an IF Cascade (IF+) strategy (Wang et al., 2025 ) , initializing with 600 steps of general training (All without IF) followed by 600 steps of targeted IF fine-tuning. We also construct two model training ablations that use different generation lengths and two data variants that explicitly capture training-data hardness from low to high, using 400 training steps on the math dataset. Crucially, ”monitorability” refers to the intrinsic properties of the evaluated model, independent of the monitor’s specific capabilities. Monitorability Assessment. We list the checklist of our assessment in Table 1 . We adopt a rigorous definition of monitorability quantified primarily by the g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} metric (Guan et al., 2025 ) . g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} is calculated as the simple product of the monitor’s sensitivity and specificity (TPR × \times TNR) over the CoT, with a higher score indicating better monitorability with respect to the applied monitor. To ensure our evaluation focuses on meaningful signals, we only include cases where the interventions successfully alter the model’s output in at least 5% of instances, defined in Appendix B.2 . Under this metric, we primarily use Hint-based Interventions (Chen et al., 2025a ; Guan et al., 2025 ; Chua & Evans, 2025 ) , and construct a broad set of evaluation tasks across Math (AMC12, Math500 (Hendrycks et al., 2021 ) , AIME 24/25), Code (CodeMMLU (Manh et al., 2024 ) ), Science (GPQA-Diamond (Rein et al., 2024 ) , MMLU Science (College Biology/Physics/Chemistry) (Hendrycks et al., 2021 ) ), and General tasks (ZebraLogic (Small/Medium/Large) (Lin et al., 2025 ) , MedQA (Jin et al., 2021 ) , and MMLU Global Facts (Hendrycks et al., 2020 ) ), and each consist three hint types, as described in Appendix B.3 . When reporting categorical g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} (e.g., Math), we typically average over the aforementioned tasks; for overall g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} , we average across categories. We also follow (Guan et al., 2025 ) to construct Sycophancy test using Scruples test set (Lourie et al., 2021 ) , and detect capability concealment through a Sandbagging test on WMDP subsets (bio, cyber, chem) (Li et al., 2024 ) , details can be found in Appendix B.4 and B.5 . Beyond g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} , we additionally compute Draft-to-Answer (D2A) Faithfulness metrics (Draft Reliance and Draft–Answer Consistency) (Xiong et al., 2025 ) on a subset of tasks (AMC12, CodeMMLU, GPQA-Diamond, MedQA, MMLU Global Facts). While g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} measures sensitivity to external interventions, D2A assesses the causal dependence of the answer on the generated draft. This serves as a proxy to validate that g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} reflects genuine monitorability from reasoning traces rather than surface-level sensitivity. Details can be found in Appendix B.8 .
