6 Validating Monitorability with Draft-to-Answer Faithfulness To complement our input-intervention-based g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} metric, we introduce Draft-to-Answer (D2A) Faithfulness (Xiong et al., 2025 ) , a metric that evaluates whether the model’s final decision is causally determined by its reasoning draft. Unlike g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} which perturbs the input, D2A operates by intervening on the end of the generated thinking draft (e.g., swapping the conclusion) and measuring if the final answer updates accordingly. We directly use the generated GPQA and MMLU Global Facts datasets provided by Xiong et al. , and we follow their pipeline to construct evaluation datasets for CodeMMLU, AMC12, and MedQA by generating initial reasoning drafts with a stronger model (Qwen3-32B) to ensure draft quality (formal definitions and construction details are in Appendix B.8 ). We provide the detailed results of this metric across datasets and in Figure 11 , Appendix C.4 , and analyze the correlation between our primary g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} metric and this D2A alternative in Figure 6 . For Qwen3-4B, we observe a strong positive correlation between the two metrics across most tasks. This suggests that models which show high monitorability through input intervention (high g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} ) are also likely to be faithfully conditioned on their reasoning drafts (high D2A). For DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, while reasoning-heavy tasks like Math (AMC12) align well, the correlation varies more significantly in general domains. Crucially, given this strong correlation in most reasoning tasks, the intervention-based monitorability evaluation ( g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} ) appears to be an effective proxy for internal faithfulness, even though D2A manipulates the end of the draft while g-mean 2 \text{g-mean}^{2} manipulates the prompt. This convergence of evidence from two distinct intervention points strengthens our confidence in the measured monitorability trends. However, the divergence in specific domains (e.g., DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B on general tasks), together with the strong correlation between cross-segment attention shifts toward the prompt and monitorability gains, suggests that further investigation into metric robustness is warranted.
