8 Conclusions We present a systematic study of how monitorability evolves during RLVR training. Our core findings reveal that (1) monitorability gains are not a universal byproduct of capability training but are strongly dependent on the data distribution; (2) mechanistically, these gains are linked to distribution sharpening and prompt-directed attention, rather than stronger dependence on reasoning traces; and (3) monitorability is related to both training and evaluation difficulty. These results refine the understanding of monitorability as a “free gift” and provide practical guidance for building more monitorable models in the future.
