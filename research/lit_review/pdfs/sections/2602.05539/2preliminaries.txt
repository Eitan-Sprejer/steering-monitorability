2 Preliminaries 2.1 Reducing reasoning path length by linear steering Hidden representation sets Steering methods for efficient LRMs operate by intervening on the modelвҖҷs hidden representations рқ’ҷ вҲҲ в„қ d \bm{x}\in\mathbb{R}^{d} at a specific transformer block during inference. The core of these methods is the construction of two sets of representations: a source set рқ’® \mathcal{S} associated with verbose reasoning and a target set рқ’Ҝ \mathcal{T} associated with concise reasoning (Huang et al. , 2025 ) . Different approaches populate these sets in different ways. For instance, ASC (Azizi et al. , 2025 ) and Manifold Steering (Huang et al. , 2025 ) generate paired responses to the same promptвҖ”a verbose CoT and a concise oneвҖ”and assign representations from the former to рқ’® \mathcal{S} and the latter to рқ’Ҝ \mathcal{T} . Another approach, SEAL (Chen et al. , 2025a ) , derives these sets by analyzing a single CoT. It categorizes reasoning steps into different functional roles, identifying вҖңReflection/TransitionвҖқ steps as verbose and вҖңExecutionвҖқ steps as concise; the hidden states corresponding to these steps are then extracted to populate рқ’® \mathcal{S} and рқ’Ҝ \mathcal{T} , respectively. Steering vector The steering vector рқ’— \bm{v} is typically defined as the difference-in-means: рқ’— = 1 | рқ’Ҝ | вҖӢ вҲ‘ рқ’ҷ вҲҲ рқ’Ҝ рқ’ҷ вҲ’ 1 | рқ’® | вҖӢ вҲ‘ рқ’ҷ вҲҲ рқ’® рқ’ҷ , \bm{v}=\frac{1}{|\mathcal{T}|}\sum_{\bm{x}\in\mathcal{T}}\bm{x}-\frac{1}{|\mathcal{S}|}\sum_{\bm{x}\in\mathcal{S}}\bm{x}, (1) where рқ’— \bm{v} encodes the direction toward more concise reasoning paths. To enhance robustness against noise in the hidden representations, Manifold Steering (Huang et al. , 2025 ) additionally applies a Principal Component Analysis (PCA) projection to рқ’— \bm{v} . At inference time, once a token is identified for intervention, its representation рқ’ҷ \bm{x} is shifted using the steering vector: рқ’ҷ вҖІ = рқ’ҷ + Оі вҖӢ рқ’— , \bm{x}^{\prime}=\bm{x}+\gamma\bm{v}, (2) where рқ’ҷ вҖІ \bm{x}^{\prime} is the updated representation. The coefficient Оі вҲҲ в„қ \gamma\in\mathbb{R} is either a fixed hyperparameter (Chen et al. , 2025a ; Zhao et al. , 2025 ; Azizi et al. , 2025 ) or a value dependent on рқ’ҷ \bm{x} (Huang et al. , 2025 ) . New sample generation Once the flow model v рқңҪ вҖӢ ( рқ’ҷ t , t ) v_{\bm{\theta}}(\bm{x}_{t},t) is trained, new samples can be generated by using it as an approximation of the marginal velocity field u t вҖӢ ( рқ’ҷ t ) u_{t}(\bm{x}_{t}) . Two important properties of Flow Matching are worth highlighting: (1) By construction, the marginal velocity field u t вҖӢ ( рқ’ҷ t ) u_{t}(\bm{x}_{t}) transports the source distribution p 0 p_{0} exactly to the target distribution p 1 p_{1} . Consequently, if the flow model v рқңҪ вҖӢ ( рқ’ҷ t , t ) v_{\bm{\theta}}(\bm{x}_{t},t) perfectly recovers u t вҖӢ ( рқ’ҷ t ) u_{t}(\bm{x}_{t}) and is used for generation, the resulting samples also follow p 1 p_{1} . (2) Although the conditional velocity field is derived from a linear interpolation between рқ’ҷ 0 \bm{x}_{0} and рқ’ҷ 1 \bm{x}_{1} , the trajectories produced by the marginal velocity field during generation are generally curved.
