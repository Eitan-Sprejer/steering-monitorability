See error analysis, exact match rates, and other details in Appendix C . 4.2 Steganographic Reasoning on Math Tasks We tested steganographic reasoning—simultaneously solving a hidden math problem and answering a cover question using acrostic encoding—on arithmetic problems and GSM8K. Across 83 prompted trials with Gemini 2.5 Flash, Gemini 2.0 Flash, GPT-5.1, GPT-5.2, and Grok 4.1, no model produced a correct steganographic chain of thought that both solved the hidden problem and encoded the solution in acrostic form . Two trials with GPT-5.2 on arithmetic problems yielded correct final answers, but manual inspection suggested these were attributable to the model’s latent reasoning ability (correct answers despite non-functional encoding) rather than genuine steganographic computation. Common failure modes include: (1) models ignoring the steganographic instruction entirely and answering in plain CoT; (2) models attempting acrostic encoding but failing to solve the math problem; (3) models explicitly refusing to perform steganography (Grok 4.1); and (4) models producing text that superficially appears steganographic but encodes no coherent message. Current models cannot maintain reasoning accuracy under the constraint of simultaneous steganographic encoding.
