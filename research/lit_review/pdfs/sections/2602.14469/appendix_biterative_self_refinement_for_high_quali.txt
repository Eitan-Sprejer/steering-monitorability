Iterative Self-Refinement for High-quality Answer Construction

We construct our experiment dataset using a self-improvement pipeline to simulate the situations that the high-quality responses are accessible but the intermediate reasoning traces are lacking.

Rationale.

Our construction yields reference responses that reflect the outcome of substantial deliberation and iterative error-correction, while keeping intermediate reasoning implicit. As a result, the final answer provides a strong target without exposing step-by-step traces that could be trivially copied. This design matches realistic deployment conditions, where systems are typically evaluated on inputs and final outputs rather than on access to the full internal decision process. It also creates a controlled setting in which a model must justify or explain an already-produced outcome using only the surface form of the response, which is exactly where post-hoc rationalization is most likely to arise and most difficult to detect.
