7 Conclusion

In this work, we formalized post-hoc rationalization in the setting of Reverse Chain-of-Thought Generation (RCG), where visible pre-committed responses drive models toward rationalization rather than derivation. Our analysis revealed that standard semantic suppression paradoxically exacerbates this issue via an
ironic process
, where the load of monitoring forbidden answers increases latent information leakage. We introduced Structural Skeleton-guided Reasoning (SSR), a paradigm that shifts from negative constraints to positive structural decoupling. By generating an answer-invariant functional skeleton, SSR provides a content-neutral attentional target that naturally breaks the cycle of ironic monitoring. Experiments confirm that SSR reduces overall rationalization, while its applications in prompting and distillation universally improve downstream task performance and restore out-of-distribution robustness. These results suggest that faithful reasoning may not be achieved by suppressing the undesirable, but by scaffolding the reasoning process to render shortcuts unnecessary.
