7 Conclusion

By disentangling knowledge existence from behavior expression, we find that hallucination and deception arise from fundamentally different internal mechanisms.
Hallucination reflects a global and irreversible absence of knowledge, whereas deception emerges from a sparse, distributed, and globally coordinated regulation of behavior under fixed knowledge.
This distinction reframes LLM reliability as a mechanism-level problem, requiring evaluation and control to distinguish missing knowledge from regulated behavior.
