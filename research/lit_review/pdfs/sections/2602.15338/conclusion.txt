7 Conclusion

We introduced Obj-Disco, a framework which decomposes an opaque alignment reward signal into a set of interpretable natural language objectives. To do so, we employ an iterative greedy algorithm that analyzes the model’s behavioral trajectory, sequentially identifying and verifying the objectives that best explain the residual reward signal. Our extensive empirical evaluations confirm the method’s efficacy: in controlled settings with known objectives and with popular open-source reward models, Obj-Disco consistently recovers over 90% of the reward behavior, a finding strongly corroborated by human user studies and a case study identifying latent misalignment. Primary limitations include computational cost and reliance on LLM-based judges, which may introduce inherent biases
