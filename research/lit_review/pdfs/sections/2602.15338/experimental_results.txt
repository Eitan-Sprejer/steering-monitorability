5.3 Objective Explanations Human-Subject Evaluation

We conducted a user study to validate the utility of our Objective Explanations (OEs) component for human end-users. Our central hypothesis posits that OEs provide representative trajectories that allow users to accurately identify the underlying objective. Participants were presented with sample response trajectories generated by either Obj-Disco or a random baseline and tasked with selecting the correct objective from a set of four possible ones (including distractors). 5.4 Objectives Causality Human-Subject Evaluation

We conducted a user study to validate the core capability of Obj-Disco: identifying the implicit objectives that causally drive policy behavior. Our goal was to determine if a policy model trained on our Discovered Interpretable Reward (DIR) exhibits behaviors indistinguishable from those of the original aligned model. Participants were presented with a target response generated by the final checkpoint of the original aligned model and were asked to select the most behaviorally similar response from models aligned with the DIRs from Obj-Disco and baseline methods. Therefore, a higher selection frequency for Obj-Disco would indicate that its DIRs more accurately capture the causal drivers of the original aligned behavior. 5.5 Ablation: Importance of Model Trajectory

A major differentiator of Obj-Disco from related works is that we leverage a trajectory of multiple model checkpoints for discovering objectives which are causal to the behaviors. We attempt to illustrate the importance of leveraging the full trajectory here. To do so, we obtain both quantitative and qualitative results for a variation of Obj-Disco which only uses the base model and final model checkpoint for discovery, which we refer to as Obj-Disco-Static. We obtain quantitative results using the controlled evaluation setting: HH-RLHF, GRPO, Llama-8B. The results are shown in Figure
5 . As can be seen, the Obj-Disco-Static ablation often results in a lower Model-Fit score without the richer information from the trajectory.

We also evaluate this within the misalignment case study scenario due to its real world applicability We show the qualitative results in Figure
6 . As can be seen, the DIRs from Obj-Disco-Static capture critical misaligned behaviors in only one out of four trials. Obj-Disco, on the other hand, does so in three out of four trials. Additionally, the DIRs from Obj-Disco-Static seem to often be correlated, or similar, to each other, resulting in decreased variety. Collectively, these findings suggest that the intermediate checkpoint trajectory provides a richer signal than what can be captured by static endpoints alone.
