6 Related Works

LLM Evaluation.

More recent research is specifically auditing the
alignment
 of LLMs. Some works audit for value alignment by analyzing model responses on interactive benchmarks and specialized datasets
(Ye et al.,
2025 ; Huang et al.,
2025 ; Murthy et al.,
2025 ; Hendrycks et al.,
2023 ; Yao et al.,
2025 ; Ren et al.,
2024 ; Jiang et al.,
2025a
; Pan et al.,
2023 )
. In a study on the feasibility of auditing, Marks et al. first induced a hidden objective into a model and then tasked separate teams with discovering it using interpretability and prompting tools
(2025)
. While insightful, these approaches typically rely on evaluating models against a pre-defined set of values or find a single, known hidden objective. Our work does not make such assumptions as developers often cannot anticipate the full range of behaviors that may emerge during alignment.

Understanding LLM Misalignment.

A significant body of research focuses on understanding misalignment
(Betley et al.,
2025 ; Entezami & Naseh,
2025 ; Liang et al.,
2023 )
. Many studies have cataloged such unintended consequences, such as producing overly long responses
(Singhal et al.,
2024 )
, reducing output diversity
(Kirk et al.,
2024 )
, degrading refusal behavior
(Song et al.,
2025 )
, and misleading humans or giving malicious advice
(Wen et al.,
2024 ; Betley et al.,
2025 ; Ghandeharioun et al.,
2024 ; Tamkin et al.,
2023 ; MacDiarmid et al.,
2025 )
. Other works have proposed to mitigate certain misalignment through techniques like causal reward modeling
(Wang et al.,
2025 )
, balanced preference datasets
(Singhal et al.,
2024 )
, or decoupling reward from confounding variables
(Shen et al.,
2023 )
. Our work instead focuses on discovery from scratch rather than mitigation or documentation of a bias.

Reward Model Interpretability.

Jiang et al. leverage controlled perturbations to interpret RMs and propose to generate contrastive explanations by modifying responses along a predefined set of attributes (e.g., helpfulness, verbosity) and observing the impact on the RM’s preference
(
2025b
)
. Christian et al. perform an exhaustive search over the RM’s vocabulary, identifying tokens that are considered most "optimal" and "pessimal" to reveal any inherent biases
(2025)
. Others have focused on decomposing a scalar reward into a multi-objective vector, making the trade-offs more explicit
(Wang et al.,
2024 ; Zhang et al.,
2025 )
. Finally, Marks et al. applied sparse autoencoders to interpret implicit RMs by analyzing features obtained from the activations between the base and fine-tuned model
(2023)
. Instead of analyzing predefined, potentially correlated attributes, we discover objectives from scratch and aim to isolate out the directional nature of alignment and maximize coverage.
